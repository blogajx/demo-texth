Biography

Sharif Osman Bin Hadi (known popularly as Osman Hadi) was a Bangladeshi political activist, writer, and teacher. Born in Nalchity Upazila, Jhalokathi District, he emerged as a significant leader during the student-led movements of the mid-2020s.


Hadi completed his education at the University of Dhaka, where he studied Political Science. He was known for his articulate speech and his role as the co-founder and spokesperson of Inqilab Moncho (Platform for Revolution), an organization established in the aftermath of the July 2024 Revolution.


As a vocal critic of foreign hegemony and authoritarianism, Hadi advocated for the rights of the "July Martyrs" and participated actively in protests demanding political reforms. By late 2025, he had become a symbol of resistance for many youths in Bangladesh.

The Events of December 2025

December 12, 2025

The Attack

At approximately 2:25 p.m., while leaving a mosque in the Paltan area of Dhaka, Sharif Osman Hadi was shot by masked assailants on a motorcycle. He was critically injured in the attack, which occurred amidst his independent campaign for the upcoming parliamentary elections.

December 15, 2025

Medical Evacuation

After initial treatment at Dhaka Medical College Hospital and Evercare Hospital, Hadi's condition remained critical. He was airlifted to Singapore General Hospital for advanced medical care.

December 18, 2025

Passing

Despite the best efforts of medical professionals, Sharif Osman Hadi succumbed to his injuries and passed away in Singapore. His death was met with an outpouring of grief and led to a "Day of State Mourning" declared by the interim government (Muhammad Yunus).

Aftermath

Nationwide Unrest

News of his death triggered widespread protests across Dhaka and other major cities. Demonstrators demanded justice, leading to clashes and attacks on various institutions. The police identified suspects linked to political opposition groups, intensifying the national debate on safety and justice.

Philosophy & Legacy

A Vision for a New Bangladesh

Beyond his role as a spokesperson, Osman Hadi was recognized as an intellectual architect for the post-2024 generation. He argued fiercely that true revolution was not just changing leaders, but dismantling systemic authoritarian structures.


His speeches often emphasized the necessity of political literacy among students and the rural youth, believing that an informed populace was the only defense against future tyrannies.

Educational Reform

Advocated for de-politicizing university campuses and revising national history curriculums.

Youth Empowerment

Believed the leadership torch must pass immediately to the generation that fought on the streets.

Anti-Hegemony

Stood firmly against external interference in Bangladesh's domestic policy.

Mostly asked question about Shaheed Sharif Osman Hadi

Sharif Osman Bin Hadi was a prominent Bangladeshi political activist, academic, and writer who emerged as a key leader in the post-2024 political landscape. He served as the spokesperson for Inqilab Moncho (Platform for Revolution). A graduate of the University of Dhaka, he was deeply respected for his articulate advocacy for national sovereignty, his role in the student movements, and his dedication to the rights of the "July Martyrs."

On December 12, 2025, Osman Hadi was the victim of a targeted assassination attempt. While leaving a mosque in the Paltan area of Dhaka after prayers at approximately 2:25 PM, he was ambushed by masked assailants on a motorcycle. He sustained critical gunshot wounds during this attack, which sent shockwaves through the nation and triggered widespread protests.

Following the attack, Hadi fought for his life for six days. He was initially admitted to Dhaka Medical College Hospital and later transferred to Evercare Hospital. Due to the severity of his condition, he was airlifted to Singapore General Hospital for advanced treatment. Despite the best efforts of medical specialists, he succumbed to his injuries and passed away on December 18, 2025.

Medically, the cause of death was multiple organ failure resulting from the critical gunshot injuries. Politically, the attack is widely believed to be a calculated effort to silence his voice. As an independent candidate and a vocal critic of foreign hegemony and authoritarianism, his growing popularity among the youth is suspected to be the primary motive behind the attack.
